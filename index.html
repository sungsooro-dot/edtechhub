JO
James Okafor Partner · EdVentures Capital
Investment

The EdTech market is consolidating fast. In the last 90 days: 3 major acquisitions, 2 significant pivots. The startups surviving? Those with strong unit economics AND genuine learning outcomes data. Vanity metrics don't close Series A rounds anymore.

View post →
SC
Sarah Chen Head of Learning Design · FutureEd Labs
AI & Learning

AI-powered adaptive learning isn't replacing teachers — it's giving them superpowers. After 6 months piloting across 40 London schools, teacher confidence in personalised learning jumped 73%. The data is clear: tech + trust = transformation.

View post →
PN
Dr. Priya Nair Director · Learning Innovation Institute
Policy & Evidence

Hot take: the biggest barrier to EdTech adoption isn't the technology. It's trust. Schools need robust evidence, not polished demos. That's why we're launching a new impact measurement framework with 12 partner institutions this autumn.

View post →
MW
Marcus Webb Founder & CEO · LearnBridge · Singapore
EdTech Startup

Just closed our Seed round. What made the difference? Not the product deck — it was our learning outcomes data. 3 years of proof that what we build actually works. Investors in EdTech are more sophisticated than people think. Show them the evidence.

View post →
AT
Aiko Tanaka Head of L&D · GlobalTech Korea · Seoul
Corporate Learning

We piloted 4 different EdTech platforms this year for upskilling our 2,000-person team. The winner wasn't the flashiest — it was the one that fit into existing workflows. Adoption is 80% about change management, 20% about the technology. Never forget that.

View post →
EH
Dr. Emma Hartley Senior Policy Adviser · Dept. for Education
Government

The UK's new EdTech strategy is promising — but implementation is everything. We've seen too many well-funded initiatives fail at the school level because teachers weren't involved in the design. Co-design with educators isn't optional. It's the whole point.
